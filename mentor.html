HackGT 7: Mentorship

We are super excited that you are interested in being a mentor at HackGT 7! Mentors are an integral part of the hackathon experience for helping participants learn and create their projects.

In order to continue building the hackathon community, we want to actively support students who are new to computer science or hackathons. This year we have created a track called Emerging Hackers, who are participants with introductory level experience in computer science but have little project experience or who are diving into their very first coding project. This is separate from the General Hackers, which consists of intermediate and advanced participants.

Hackathons can be intimidating for Emerging hackers, so part of making their hackathon experience a success is having regular support from someone to help answer questions and guide them through (for most) their first hackathon project. This is what drives our mentorship program this year.

Mentors for Emerging Hacker Teams

We have created the Emerging Hacker Mentorship program, which is made so Emerging hacker teams (teams with majority emerging hackers) can have a designated mentor to help guide them through the hackathon. If you join this program, you will:

  • Be matched to 1-2 emerging hacker teams based on their goals and your expertise at the beginning of the event
  • Check-in with team at least once each day of the hackathon (3 meetings)
  • Answer questions and give advice to your assigned team, such as technical questions and assisting with their devpost submissions and presentations.

In addition to helping your teams, you still have the opportunity to help other general hackers! This is an optional program. If you are interested in being a mentor for an Emerging Hacker team, let us know in the Mentorship Application. Once accepted and confirmed, we will provide you with more information and resources about how you can support your team.

Overall Expectations

We do not expect you to be available for all hours of the event. Once you have confirmed that you will mentor at HackGT 7, you can let us know at what times you plan to be available. In order for HackGT 7 mentors to support the participants throughout the virtual hackathon, all mentors, in the Emerging Hacker Program or not, are expected to:

  • Be available online for 4-8 hours during the event to support the participants through answering questions or helping at workshops
  • Be proactive and responsive to questions from participants using our Mentorship questions tool
  • Attend a 1-2 hour mentor training session prior to the event where we will show you how to use our tools and resources

While you offer your knowledge, as an attendee you will also have access to sponsors, workshops, mini-events, speakers, and of course, free swag!

How to Apply

Anyone at least 18 years of age may apply to be a mentor. You can be a student, professor, or industry professional. To apply, go to the Mentor application on registration.hack.gt
